						 Gampaha | Hamilton Canal 
						 
						 Hamilton Canal 
						 

	Boat Riding , Photography, Filming 

 

	 

 

	An incident which led to the canal been baptized as the Hamilton’s folly or the fool’s canal. Contradictory to the popular belief Hamilton Canal was not a creation of the King of Kotte nor the Dutch but was a later addition by Garvin Hamilton, British Agent of Revenue and Commerce in 1802, who constructed a new canal to the east of the canals created by Sinhalese king and the Dutch. 

 
						 More 
						 

	The canal was designed to connect the Dutch canal by a series of parallel canals and was designed to drain the already damaged Muturajawela but caused the opposite to occur as the high tide brought salinity not only from Negambo lagoon but also the Kelani river.
